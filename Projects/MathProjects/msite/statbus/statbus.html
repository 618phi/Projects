Mean and Medium Explained

In a set of data the mean is found by adding all the data up and then
dividing by the number of individual pieces of data in the set.

In a set of data the medium is found by placing all the individual data
in order of magnitude and selecting the middle piece of data.

If you record the number of people getting on a bus over a journey of 5 stops.
First stop, 3 people get on the bus.
Second stop, 2 people get on the bus.
Third stop, 4 poeple get on the bus.
Fourth stop, 1 person gers on the bus.
Fifth stop, 5 people get on the bus.

Putting the data in order of magnitude gives; 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.

Mean = (sum of all data) / (number of idividual pieces of data)
∴ Mean = (1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5) / 5 = 15 / 5 = 3

The median is the middle value of all the values.
If there are two middle values, add the two values and divide by two.
Here, he medium is the third largest piece of data. ∴ medium = 3
